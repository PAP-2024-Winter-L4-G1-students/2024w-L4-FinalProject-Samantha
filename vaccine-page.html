How Do Vaccines Work?

  • mRNA vaccines such as the Pfizer-BioNTech and Moderna vaccines work by teaching the cells in the body how to make a protien that triggers an immune response. This is done by administering a weakened pathogen into the body and an immunse system response is acvivated. Your cells make the S protien that is found on the COVID-29 virus.
  • Protien subunit vaccines such as the Novavax vaccine work by including only the parts of a virus that best stimulate your immune system. S protiens are included which causes your immunse system to build antibodies.
  • Both vaccines make it easier on your immune system to attack and get rid of the SARS-CoV-2 virus. When your immune system comes into contact with this pathogen again, it will be better equipped.
